b) DSB-LC requires a carrier because conventional AM uses the carrier as the reference that enables simple envelope detection at the receiver. DSB-SC removes the carrier to save power, so it does not carry information by itself and must be demodulated coherently with a synchronized local oscillator.

c) The main difference in the spectra is that DSB-LC has a large carrier spike at the center frequency, while DSB-SC suppresses that carrier and leaves only the upper and lower sidebands around the carrier frequency.
